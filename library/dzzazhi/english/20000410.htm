1......China Aids warning
BBC News,  Wednesday,April 5

A senior leader in China is calling for the fight against Aids in the country to be made a national priority.

Deputy prime minister and Politburo member Li Lanqing told a conference in Beijing that government should spend more time and money on an issue that was he said vital to the fate of China.

Officials said the number of registered HIV cases grew 30% last year, along with a big growth in sexually-transmitted diseases.

Chinese health experts put current estimates for the total number with HIV at 500,000.

2......Japan PM plans a smooth transition
Sydney Morning Herald News, Thursday, April 6

Japan's new leader, Mr Yoshiro Mori, yesterday vowed not to soft-pedal on structural reforms after being installed as the nation's 27th postwar prime minister.

Stung by criticism that he was being handed a figurehead role in the run-up to the next election, Mr Mori, virtually unknown outside political circles a few days ago, said he was intent on maintaining the "economic revitalisation" policies of his predecessor, Mr Keizo Obuchi.

In a swift succession of events, Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) members rubber-stamped Mr Mori's appointment as party president, clearing the way for his election as prime minister in the Diet an hour later.

The vote was along party lines, with Mr Mori, 62, gaining a clear majority in both houses because of the LDP's alliance with the Buddhist-backed New Komeito and the new Conservative Party.

Late yesterday his new Cabinet - identical Mr Obuchi's, save for the change of leaders - was sworn in at a ceremony at the Imperial Palace. Mr Mori had made it clear he would keep the Cabinet intact to ensure a smooth transition.

3......Microsoft penalty in 60 days
BBC News, Friday, April 7

Software giant Microsoft has agreed to a speedy sentencing process after it was ruled to have broken US monopoly laws.

The Seattle-based firm and the US Justice Department agreed to fast track the penalty setting phase, after a private meeting with the trial judge Thomas Penfield Jackson.

US District Judge Jackson is reported to have tentatively scheduled the start of penalty-setting hearings for 24 May.

During a private meeting, he gave attorneys for the Justice Department and the 19 states suing Microsoft until the end of this month to submit their proposed remedies.

He gave Microsoft slightly less than two weeks to respond to that proposal, and the government another week for a rebuttal.

Judge Jackson also told those involved that he wanted to "fast track" the appeal directly to the Supreme Court to avoid delays that could "disrupt the economy".

He said he wanted to get the case to an appeal court as quickly as possible, perhaps straight to the Supreme Court, in as few as 60 days.

"My transcendent objective is to get this thing before an appellate tribunal - one or another - as quickly as possible because I don't want to disrupt the economy or waste any more of yours or my time," the judge said according to a transcript of the meeting released late on Tuesday.

4......Nasdaq posts record gain
CNNfn News, Friday, April 07

Technology stocks soared Friday, handing the Nasdaq composite index its biggest point gain on record and capping a bruising week on Wall Street.

The tech recovery kept the Dow Jones industrial average from suffering big losses in a quiet, orderly session that came as welcome contrast to the previous days.

"In the past four days prior to Friday we packed in two weeks worth of work," John Pickett, of LaBranche & Co. told CNN's Street Sweep. "On Tuesday people didn't just get hit; they got decked."

The Nasdaq Tuesday plunged 574 points, or 13.5 percent, before partially recovering. A day earlier the gauge fell nearly 350 points, a record.

But the index rose three times in the last three sessions, ahead of what should be a strong season for companies reporting earnings for the first three month of 2000.

1......Love adds the Chocolate
A house is a house is a house-until love comes through the door, that is. And love intuitively goes around sprinkling that special brand of angel dust that transforms a house into a very special home for very special people: your family.

Money, of course, can build a charming house, but only love can furnish it with a feeling of home.

Duty can pack an adequate sack lunch, but love may decide to tuck a little love note inside.

Money can provide a television set, but love controls it and cares enough to say no and take the guff that comes with it.

Obligation sends the children to bed on time, but love tucks the covers in around their necks and passes out kisses and hugs (even to teenagers!).

Obligation can cook a meal, but love embellishes the table with a potted ivy trailing around slender candles.

Duty writes many letters, but love tucks a joke or a picture or a fresh stick of gum inside.

Compulsion keeps a sparkling house. But love and prayer stand a better chance of producing a happy family.

Duty gets offended quickly if it isn't appreciated. But love learns to laugh a lot and to work for the sheer joy of doing it.

Obligation can pour a glass of milk, but quite often love will add a little chocolate.

1......Deadwater

Deadwater

- Salvatore Quasimodo (1901-1968)

Closed water, sleep of the marshes
broad-streaked, soaking poison,
now white, now green in lightning flashes,
you are like my heart.
Around, the grey of poplar and ilex,
leaves and acorns still within,
each with its uni-centred circles
frayed by the dark southwester drone.

So as on water memory
spreads its widening rings, my heart
moves from one point out and dies,
sister to you, deadwater.

1......Dubai Story (4)
以下是网易网友zhangxiaoquan的Dubai Story，英语杂志将在今后几期里连载。希望其他尝试英文写作的朋友也将作品寄来，让我们一起将这个栏目办好。

Johnson and the guy followed me out. We were stared by all the people in the temple. There came a fat guy who have just washed his feet. When he learned we were "Chinese Muslim", he shook my hands violently, I tried to squeeze out some smile while holding his wet hands.

The fat guy asked me, "what is your Muslim name?" I answered in the same way and he asked me how many Muslims there were in China. I answered: "several million". He laughed as if I were joking, then asked "How long have you become a Muslim?" That was hard for me, does this suggest people should be over some rank to enter this temple? I answered: "I followed my parents, so that's many years." My leg was soft and I couldn't bear to concoct one more single lie to these curious people.

We said bye to them politely and walked as calm as possible out of the temple and stopped the first taxi we saw and rushed away. I looked back in the rear mirror of the car, the guys are still looking at us. I could not find the peasant-looking Indian at the door, I wanted to slap his face.

We were told before that there was a place called Jumpier Beach which was very beautiful. So we went there. It took us 30 minutes to go there. we saw even more well-decorated villas on the road, most of them are two-storied and white. We can only see BMW, Mercedes, NISSAN, RANGE CRUISER... on the road, we are entering to the habitation of the rich.

Many Arabian people just dressed in the white cloth with a white scarf on the head and two black rings to fasten the scarf. They are well-shaved and the cloths were very very clean, they look like, in a sense, angles. There was a hotel named "Jumpier Gulf Tower", it was the first 7-star hotel in the world. It costs 54,000 DHS for one night, just calculate the rate in USD, your tongue will come out of the mouth.

We caught the sun set from the beach, it was such a romantic place that Johnson and I just sit there speechless. Maybe some of my photos could describe the sense. It was breathtaking.
