1......Uganda Authorities Search New Graves
AP News, Friday, March 31

Ugandan authorities today unearthed a dozen new bodies from graves in a fourth compound connected to a Christian doomsday sect, and officials were questioning a former district official about the deaths of sect members in a fire.

In their latest search for victims of a secretive religious sect, police were concentrating on a hilltop village in southwestern Uganda where a neighbor said people would regularly ``vanish.''

Authorities said they had detained for questioning a former official in Kanungu sub-district where the church fire occurred two weeks ago.

2......EU may allow delay to market opening
South China Morning Post News, Friday, March 31

The European Union may let Beijing delay market-opening measures for a decade or more to win key trade concessions such as majority ownership of mainland joint ventures, according to an EU official.

This has come after mainland and EU negotiators ended a third day of gruelling WTO talks yesterday without an agreement but with the EU sounding positive over the prospects for a deal.

The 15-nation bloc is pushing Beijing to let foreign companies own more than 50 per cent of domestic ventures - especially in the lucrative telecommunications and insurance markets - as a condition for backing its World Trade Organization entry.

Beijing had not indicated whether it would accept the extended transition periods, the EU official said.

It has so far refused to budge from the 50 per cent cap it put on foreign ownership in its WTO agreement with the US in November.

3......Porn video parlour's door locked as 74 died in fire
South China Morning Post News, Friday, March 31, 2000

The owner of the video parlour where 74 people were killed in a fire early on Wednesday locked the door to avoid police raids during pornographic video shows, according to mainland media.

The victims failed to escape as the main entrance of Tiantang Cinema - known for showing pornographic videos in the early morning - was locked from the outside when the fire broke out, according to the Yangcheng Evening News yesterday, adding it explained the unusually high death toll.

It said there were about 90 people - mostly migrant workers - in the cinema in Jiaozuo city, Henan, when the fire erupted at 3am. Only a few could escape by pushing open a side door leading to a cinema next door.

Many members of the audience who fell asleep in the boxes were choked to death without even attempting to escape, the paper quoted witnesses as saying.

4......Hackers Hold Convention in Israel
AP News, Thursday, March 30

Hackers from around the world overcame interrogations, censorship and an all-around bad image to hold Israel's first hacker convention, wrapping up the two-day conference Thursday without a glitch.

The 350-strong gathering was the first of its kind since the Yahoo! and eBay commercial sites were crippled in February, reminding companies across the globe of the dangers hackers can pose.

At the request of lawmakers, Israeli police had considered banning the conference, but Attorney General Eliyakim Rubinstein gave the go-ahead.

One of the original hackers, John Draper of Fremont, Calif., said the hackers wanted to put a better face on the practice.

``A hacker is a person who is developing programs to make them better,'' Draper said. ``They aren't the kind of people who break into computer systems. That's a cracker.''

2......Did you know? (14)
Of all the symbols associated with Easter the egg, the symbol of fertility and new life, is the most identifiable. The customs and traditions of using eggs have been associated with Easter for centuries

Originally Easter eggs were painted with bright colors to　represent the sunlight of spring and were used in Easter-egg rolling contests or given as gifts. After they were colored and etched with various designs the eggs were exchanged by lovers and romantic admirers, much the same as valentines.

1......On the Nature of Love

On the Nature of Love

From Chaitali (1896)

The night is black and the forest has no end;
a million people thread it in a million ways.
We have trysts to keep in the darkness, but where
or with whom- of that we are unaware.
But we have this faith- that a lifetime's bliss
will appear any minute, with a smile upon its lips.
Scents, touches, sounds, snatches of songs
brush us, pass us, give us delightful shocks.
Then peradventure there's a flash of lightning:
whomever I see that instant I fall in love with.
I call that person and cry: `This life is blest!
For your sake such miles have I traversed!'
All those others who come close and moved off
in the darkness- I don't know if they exist or not.

1......Word & Phrase Study (32)
the tune the old cow died of

the tune the old cow died of是英国的一句俗语，意思是空言无补。出自英国的一首古老的歌曲，这首歌讲的是一则寓言故事:

there was an old man, & he had an old cow,
but he had no fodder to give her,
so he took up his fiddle & played her the tune;
" consider, good cow, consider,
this isn't time for the grass to grow,
consider, good cow, consider. "
of course, the old cow died of the tune!

例句： 哦，算了，不要再给我这些毫无用处的忠告了。

oh, no. not more of the tune the old cow died of!

2......汉英虚词学习(40)
好歹

I.表示好坏

1.他这人简直布置好歹。
He simply doesn't know what is good and what is bad for him.

2.你怎么敢问他这样一个问题，真是不知好歹！
How dare you ask him such a question! You should have known how to behave yourself.

3.万一他有个好歹，你我都得担关系。
You and I are to be held responsible if anything should happen to him.

II.表示无论如何

1.我也知道这饼做得不好，你就好歹尝一尝吧。
I know the cake is not very good, but have a try, anyway.

2.“太公放心，我好歹要救你女儿还你。”
"Don't worry, old man! At any rate, I'll save your daughter and return her to you!"

3.不管怎么说，这好歹是辆新车子。
Say what you like; it's a new car.

1......Dubai Story (3)
以下是网易网友zhangxiaoquan的Dubai Story，英语杂志将在今后几期里连载。希望其他尝试英文写作的朋友也将作品寄来，让我们一起将这个栏目办好。

Coming out of the museum, we found a large Muslim temple not far away. I was curious about the inside architecture, so asked some farmer-looking guy if we could go inside. He shook his head (the way Indian people say "yes"). I asked him again, he said "yes". We then took off our shoes and put them outside the temple and went in.

It was a rectangular yard, on the left there was a heavy door; on the right side, there was a room with pipes looking like a WC, later I was told it was used to wash the feet before going into the rooms. In front of us, there was a huge door leading to a big room. We took the left side to check out the smaller room. It was actually a hall, with beautiful poles and golden shelves for books. The floor was glint stone, and shining like mirror. There were several guys praying in the hall.

We were stunned by the beauty of the hall. Then suddenly, someone came up to ask me: "where are you from?" As usual, I smiled friendly and replied: "China". He did not smile like the other people we met in the street. Rather, he asked a question and scared me: "Are you Muslim?" I was smart enough to answer: "Yes". (I heard that the temples were restricted to the Muslims) He stared at me with disbelief: "Do you want to pray?" "Ah.. yes..." Then I suddenly understand that I do not know how to pray, then added: " we have already done that.. heihei". The sheepish smile did not conceal my nervousness.

He directed the eyesight to Johnson, Johnson showed an innocent face as if saying :" I ... I ... speak English not, sorry.. I don't .. understand". He was really smart, huh? The Muslim then looked at me again and asked another question that frightened me to death: "What is your Muslim name?" I wanted to say:"Muhammad" or "Ali Ababa"(Like the name of Aladdin) because I know many Muslim was called that way, but my knowledge was too limited, I did not know what would happen if I say so. Suppose the name is only for some messenger like "Jesus", I can foresee two endings:

1. The guy pulling out an axe to chop my tongue for telling the lie.

2. He getting on the knees and say "Allah, my all mighty God" to me.

I like neither result. So I just smiled sheepishly again and told him: "Actually, we have Chinese Muslim names...xixi.. and we read Chinese version of Quran...xixi...Oh...do you want to pray?" He stared at us and saw my camera, I explained hopelessly:" I did not take pictures here" then starting to move outside the hall.
