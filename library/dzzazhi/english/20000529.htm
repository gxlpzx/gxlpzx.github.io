1......FBI: New Computer Virus Spreading
AP News, Saturday, May 27

The computer e-mail viruses that swept the world earlier this month claimed to be looking for love, but now a new threat has arrived looking for a job.

A new and dangerous computer virus dubbed ``Killer Resume'' is spreading through e-mail systems using the Microsoft Outlook program, the FBI said.

Computer security companies reported that several corporate e-mail systems had already been infected, and some shut down as a result, the FBI's National Infrastructure Protection Center said Friday night.

The virus is known as the ``Killer Resume'' because it arrives pretending to be a resume from a potential job applicant, the anti-virus company Network Associates said on their Web site. The virus is carried in a file attached to an e-mail with the subject ``Resume - Janet Simons.''

2......S. Leone Rebels Release UN Hostages
AP News, Sunday, May 28

Sierra Leonean rebels on Sunday freed what appeared to be the last of some 500 U.N. hostages seized earlier this month, signaling ``a positive change of direction'' among the rebels toward bringing peace to the war-torn West African nation, a U.N. spokesman said.

Eighty-five former captives arrived in Freetown Sunday night from Monrovia, the capital of neighboring Liberia, said U.N. spokesman David Wimhurst.

A senior Liberian military official who spoke on condition he not be named said more freed hostages were still on the Sierra Leone-Liberia border and would be flown out on Monday. Wimhurst could not confirm whether there were more hostages awaiting evacuation from the remote area.

Four peacekeepers unaccounted for were believed to have been killed in fighting when the hostage crisis began on May 6, Wimhurst said.

3......Facts About China Trade Bill
AP News, Wednesday, May 24

-Would give China permanent normal trade relations, the same low-tariff privileges the United States routinely accords its other trading partners. For the past 20 years, China has gotten these same benefits - formerly ``most-favored-nation'' status - but subject to annual renewal.

-Would enable U.S. companies to take full advantage of a landmark market-opening pact negotiated with China as part of its entry into the World Trade Organization. The trade pact would reduce average Chinese tariffs, abolish Chinese import quotas and licenses and allow foreign businesses to invest in Chinese banking, telecommunications and other companies.

-Provisions unique to the House bill: sets up an executive-legislative watchdog commission to monitor human rights and trade conduct in China and report annually to Congress; puts in force a mechanism to protect certain U.S. industries against surges in Chinese imports; includes a $99 million boost for international broadcasting programs, including Radio Free Asia and the Voice of America.

-Support for the bill: The Clinton administration has made it a top priority and corporate America has waged an intensive lobbying campaign. Sponsors claim the bill will open China's vast markets to U.S. companies and promote democratic change by exporting American values.

-Opposition to the bill: From a coalition of labor, environmental, some human-rights groups and the religious right. Opponents claim the measure would reward a brutal regime. Unions fear liberalized trade would make it easier for U.S. manufacturers to move factories to China and take advantage of low-wage workers.

4......Asian Films Steal Cannes Festival Spotlight
Reuters News, Friday, May 19

Whether they get a Golden Palm or not, Asian directors are the clear winners of this year's Cannes Film Festival, serving up a feast of films that combine originality and a particular brand of sensitivity that is all their own.

With an unprecedented six films in competition, this flourishing of Far Eastern cinema has its origins in the 1990s, when Chinese productions first became known to a large public. In 1993, Chen Kaige's ``Farewell My Concubine'' became the first Chinese film to win the Golden Palm.

This year at Cannes, three Chinese, two Japanese and the first ever Korean film in competition are stealing the show.

``Devils on the Doorstep,'' from China, astonished festival-goers with its passionate and comic tale of deep-seated distrust and incomprehension between China and Japan ending in mass slaughter.

2......Did you know? (21)
The poem below captures well the fact that much of the way we choose to spend our time is drawn from our perspective on time itself If we think we have a great deal of time, we tend to waste it. If we think time is short, we value every moment.

When as a child I laughed and wept -Time crept!

When as a youth I dreamed and talked - Time walked!

When I became a full-grown man - Time ran!

Then as with the years I older grew - Time flew!

Soon I shall find as I travel on - Time gone!

Ralph Waldo Emerson once had this to say about living each moment fully: "One of the illusions of life is that the present hour is not the critical, decisive hour. Write it on your heart that every day is the best day of the year. He only is rich who owns the day, and no one owns the day who allows it to be invaded with worry, fret, and anxiety. Finish every day, and be done with it. You have done what you could."

1......Word & Phrase Study (39)
the die is cast

the die is cast是木已成舟、大局以定的意思。据牛津词典记载，“the die is cast”，这一成语是指骰子巳经掷出，犹如覆水难收一般。 这一成语在1592年上演的莎士比亚著名历史剧<<查理三世>> 中就以不同的形式出现过: “I have set my life upon the cast, and I will stand the hazard of the die. ” (我巳经把我这条命打过赌，我宁可孤注一掷，决个胜负。朱生豪译)

At all events, what use was there in delaying? the die is cast(thrown) and now or tomorrow the issue must be the same.

不管怎么样，拖下去有什么好处呢？反正木已成舟，今天说穿和明天说穿还不是一样。

1......Share
A reporter once asked a farmer to divulge the secret behind his corn, which won the state fair contest year after year. The farmer confessed it was all because he shared his seed with his neighbors.

"Why do you share your best seed corn with your neighbors when you're entering the same contest each year as well?" asked the reporter.

"Why sir," said the farmer, "didn't you know? The wind picks up pollen from the ripening corn and swirls it from field to field. If my neighbors grew inferior corn, cross-pollination would steadily degrade the quality of my corn. If I am to grow good corn, I must help my neighbor do the same."

And so it is with other situations in our lives.
Those who want to be successful must help their neighbors be successful. Those who choose to live well must help others live well, for the value of a life is measured by the lives it touches. And those who choose to be happy must help others find happiness, for the welfare of each is bound up with the welfare of all.
