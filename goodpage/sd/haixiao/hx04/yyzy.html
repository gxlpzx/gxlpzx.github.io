South Africa

Excerpted by HeFei and Zhu·jiyie

Nine languages, more than thirty different ethnic groups, thirty million people, several kings, two capitals, a real country? Yes, South Africa has a bit of every country from around the world. In the very South of Africa, two oceans, the Atlantic Ocean and the Indian Ocean meet at the tip of the south coast. The seas on the east coast are warm, on the west coast are cold. In the west there are great deserts and in the east there are the most beautiful gardens in the world.

  South Africa is one of Africa's most popular tourist destinations. Why do millions of tourists visit South Africa every year? There are the big safari parks, the Kruger National Park is bigger than Holland. In the safari park you drive your own car, and sheep at night in a safe area away from the animals. Animals? Lions, elephants, giraffes, impala (a kind of deer), baboons, leopards, buffalo, crocodiles all live free in the park. You need to take your binoculars along with you, you can watch the lions feeds, the elephants romp and the cheetahs sprint.

  South Africa has more gold and diamonds than anywhere else in the world. The gold comes from the Witwatersrand, an area around Johannesburg, the biggest city in Africa. Diamonds come from a town called Kimberly, where the "Kimberly diamond", the biggest diamond in the world was found. It now belongs to the Queen of England. If you go to Kimberly or Johannesburg you can take a trip in a real gold or diamond mine! There's a rule though, any gold or diamonds you find must be given back to the mine.

  Inside South Africa there are some small kingdoms, with their own kings and government. One is called Swazilang, where the Swazi people live. Their biggest city is called Sun City, which is a little like Macau, with a lot of casinos.

  On the south coast is a province called Natal. Natal is very beautiful, with the warm Indian ocean, big green forests and it's always summer. Most of the people who live in Natal are Zulus. The Zulus used to be a fierce warrior tribe, and you can still watch their dances or buy some of their handicrafts.

  South Africa probably has the best sea-food in the world, with two big oceans and miles of coast. Prawns are as big as lobsters, mussels as big as plates. There are wines from the Cape Province, and lots and lots of meat. You can eat almost any kind of animal in South Africa, you can have ostrich eggs for breakfast, dried crocodile meat for lunch and grilled fish for supper.

  If you want to go to South Africa you'll find it's very cheap, especially if you can drive a car. most people who go to South Africa often take a tent, so that they don't have to always live in hotels. There are special tours, called "package tours" where they will show you many different places in just one or two week.

Achieving One's Hopes and Goals

Kefei

What is success? Success is the realization of one's hopes and justified ideals. Under such a general notion, however, people have different understandings of success because the hopes and ideals they cherish vary one another.

  Here let me tell you a story of a young female college student.

  Not long ago, she caught a terrible nerve disease known as facial paralysis. All of sudden the left side of her face got paralyzed, her mouth slid to one side, her eye couldn't close even slightly, and the muscles were completely stiff. Like a bolt from the blue, she was thrown into a total panic.

  What to do? With her parents, the student had visited all the hospitals in the city and tried every possible means, but none worked. As time went by, the situation became more and more urgent because the curable stage was slipping by quickly. She was so scared, but deep in her heart she still kept such a tiny sparkle of hope that things would be going better one day.

  At last she found her savior, miraculously. She was introduced to a venerable retired acupuncturist. The first time the acupuncturist saw her, he was also quite uncertain if there would be any hope, but still he accepted her. From that day on, the two started their long journey of treatment.

  After one month, those little silver needles did work marvelously! Some muscles of her face were able to move a little. Another month passed. Change could be seen on her eyelid. Step by step, after six months of continuous acupuncture treatment, she got better and recovered basically at last.

  On day of her leaving, the girl and her father presented a precious gift to the acupuncturist. It's a picture frame with the most sacred six-word blessing from her ethnic nationality: Om-Ma-Ni-Pad-Me-Hung.

  Some little silver needles, a traditional Chinese acupuncturist and an ordinary college girl, together made the miracle happen! That is success.

  Talking about success, what usually comes to our mind are those great achievements and shining cases. But to me, success is just in each time we rise up to challenges.

  Why is success so dear to everybody? Because it is not easy to come by, because in the process of our striving for success, we have both our body and soul tempered, and we are enlightened by the most valuable qualities of human beings: confidence, love, courage, fortitude, patience and sense of responsibility. These are the best treasures a person can ever possess, and these have the greatest powers for us to strive for truth and happiness.

  My dear friends, now can you guess who that college student is?

  It's me, who is standing here and speaking to you in earnest and sincerity. I'm so proud that I can take part in this competition within a year after I conquered such an obstinate disease. I am also proud that I come here today not only on behalf of myself and my university, but also on behalf of my successful family, a mixed family formed by a Tibetan father and a Han mother. I am so happy that I can do credit to my ethnic originality and that land which had reared and educated me.

  What is success? Everybody has his own interpretation just as I do. But there is one thing for sure: that is every success leads you to an ever brighter future.

  My friends, believe in yourselves that you can make the best of yourselves.

  Wish you all good success.

  Thank you.

  By Kefei, the second prize winner of the "21st Century Cup" English-language speaking competition on March 31, for her speeches on " My definition of Success."

You Can't Change the Future

Excerpted by HeFei

Bill Evans was a professional baseball player. He was a good player and had played in the World Series many times.

  He was also superstitious. Before every game, he went to a fortune-teller to find out what was going to happen. One day, on the eve of an important game, he visited his favorite fortune-teller, as usual.

  She had told his fortune many times in the past and had often been correct.

   "I've got an important game tomorrow," he told her. " What's going to happen?" The fortune-teller studied his hand for a few moments. Then she said, " I see a man wearing a white coat."

  "Do you see anything else?" Bill asked.

  "Yes. I see you lying on a stretcher."

  This really worried Bill. There were often injuries during a game, but they were really serious enough for the player to be carried off on a stretcher.

  "Then I won't play tomorrow." he told the fortune-teller. "I can't risk a serious injury."

  The captain of bill's team was not pleased when he told him that he was not going to play. Bill was one of the best players on the team. However, there was nothing he could say to make Bill change his mind.

  "I'm not going to risk a serious injury," he said.

  Bill decided not to watch the game, but to go to the hospital and visit one of the other players who had been badly injured a few weeks before.

  As he was walking into the hospital, a doctor wearing a white coat ran out of the door and, not seeing Bill, knocked him down. Bill hit his head on the ground and lost consciousness. Within a few seconds, he was being carried into the hospital on a stretcher.

Mid-Autumn Day

Tina, Class 107

  Mid-Autumn Day is one of the most important festivals in China. The whole family will get together eating moon cakes, all kinds of fruit and enjoying the moon. The moon is very beautiful that day with a round face and bright shining. Our faces will be full of smiling as well as the moon.

  There is a magic story about this festival.

  It's said that once there was a devil who brought diseases and suffering to the people in a small town. One day, a man and his partners made a large quantity of round cakes, each of them were put a small long piece of paper inside. The words in the paper told every one who got the cake that they must join together to kill the devil, also told how to and when and where. The people hate the devil very deeply. So they started to take up guns and other tools. At last they won to kill the devil. The people lived a happy life after it.

  In order to commemorate the hero, who join them together and killed the devil, they fixed that day as Mid-Autumn, the special food named moon cake.

How did the idea of Santa Claus originate?

He Fei, Class 104

  Long before Christ was born, people used to celebrate the winter solstice as the birth of the sun. This time of the year was a holiday in many parts of the world before it became the Christmas celebration. That's why some of the customs and traditions of Christmas go back to pagan time. The custom of giving presents goes back to the ancient Romans. In the Bible, the wise men brought gifts to Jesus on the 12th day after his birth. And so in some countries, the children receive their presents not on Christmas, but 12 days later. In some of northern countries of Europe, the gifts are exchanged almost three weeks before Christmas. The reason for this is that the gifts are supposed to be brought in by Saint Nicholas in the eve of his feast day, December the 6th. Saint Nicholas was a bishop of the 4th century who came to be regarded as a special friend of the children. So in various countries around the world Saint Nicholas returns every year with gifts for good children. When the Dutch came to New York, they brought the tradition of Saint Nicholas with them. They called him " San Nicholas." And this soon was changed to " Sankt Klaus", and then Santa Claus. But in this century we moved the date of his arrival to Christmas Eve. And gradually his red costume, the reindeer, and his home at the north pole became part of tradition.

Why do we celebratethe new year?

He Fei, Class 104

  One of the oldest customs of mankind is the celebration of the new year. Some people say the Chinese were the first to start it, others believe it was the ancient Germans, and still others claim it was Romans. We know that the Chinese have always had a great festival at the time of their new year which comes later than ours. The ancient Germans established a new year festival because of the changing seasons. The German winter began about the middle of November. This was the time when they gathered the harvest, because everybody came together at this time for the happy occasion, and because it meant they would have a period of rest from work afterwards. they would make merry and have a great holiday. Even though it was November, they considered this time of celebration is the first of January. From then the coming of the new year was a symbol of a new life with new hope for the future. This custom and this meaning has lasted to this day. We celebrate the new year hoping it will bring us a good life!
